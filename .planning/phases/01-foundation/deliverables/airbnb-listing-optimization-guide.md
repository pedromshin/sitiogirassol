## 1. Photo Audit and Selection

### Goal

Replace the current photo set with a curated selection of 25-30 photos from the 267 available in `marketing/airbnb_sitio images/`. The photos are named DSC_XXXX.JPG (camera photos, sequentially numbered).

### Hero Photo Requirement

The first photo uploaded becomes the hero/cover photo. It **must** be a golden-hour exterior or pool shot -- bright, warm-toned, inviting, and showing the property's best feature. Look for photos taken during late afternoon with warm sunlight on the pool or main building.

Browse the `marketing/airbnb_sitio images/` folder and select photos for each category below. Write down the DSC_XXXX.JPG number for each selection.

| 8 | **Bathrooms** | 2-3 photos | Clean, well-lit. Show the full bathroom. Towels folded neatly. |

### Photo Quality Tips

- **Skip:** Blurry, dark, cluttered, or poorly composed photos
- **Prefer:** Landscape orientation over portrait (displays better on Airbnb)
- **Prefer:** Rooms that look spacious (wide-angle shots)
- **Avoid:** Photos with people in them (Airbnb policy discourages this for listing photos)
- **Avoid:** Photos with visible mess, personal items, or clutter
- **Brightness:** If a photo is too dark, it will hurt conversions. Only select well-lit photos.

## 2. Listing Settings Changes

### Section A: Minimum Stay (Set to 2 Nights)

**Why:** 1-night stays have the lowest revenue per cleaning cycle. With the R$180 cleaning fee, a 1-night stay at R$280 yields only R$100 after cleaning (and only R$84 net after Airbnb's 16% host fee). A 2-night minimum ensures every booking is profitable.

**Steps:**

1. Go to **Airbnb Host Dashboard**
2. Click **Listing**
3. Click **Availability**
4. Find **Trip length** section
5. Set **Minimum night stay** to **2 nights**
6. Save changes

**Gap-Fill Exception:** If you have a single open night between two bookings (e.g., a Monday gap between a Sunday checkout and Tuesday check-in), you can temporarily reduce the minimum to 1 night for just that date:

1. Go to **Calendar**
2. Select the specific gap date(s)
3. Click **Edit**
4. Set **Minimum stay override** to 1 night for that date range only
5. This does not change the global 2-night minimum

### Section B: Advance Notice (Set to 1 Day)

**Why:** Same-day bookings do not allow enough time to prepare the property (cleaning, restocking, setup). A 1-day advance notice gives you time to arrange cleaning and ensure the property is ready.

**Steps:**

1. Go to **Airbnb Host Dashboard**
2. Click **Listing**
3. Click **Availability**
4. Find **Advance notice** section
5. Set to **1 day** (recommended) or **2 days** if you need more preparation time
6. Save changes

**Note:** If you find that 1 day is too tight for arranging cleaning, increase to 2 days. The tradeoff is that 2 days blocks some last-minute bookings but gives you more preparation comfort.

### Section C: Categories (Verify Countryside and Tropical)

**Why:** Airbnb categories help guests discover your property through category browsing (not just search). Being in the right categories means showing up when guests browse "Countryside" or "Tropical" -- both are perfect matches for Sitio Girassol.

**Steps:**

1. Go to **Airbnb Host Dashboard**
2. Click **Listing**
3. Click **Property and rooms**
4. Find **Property type and categories**
5. Verify these are selected:
   - **Property type:** "Farm stay" or "Entire home" (whichever best describes the sitio)
   - **Countryside** category: Must be checked
   - **Tropical** category: Must be checked
6. Also check for these additional categories if available in your region:
   - **Pools** -- the pool is a major selling point
   - **Amazing views** -- if the property qualifies
7. Save any changes

**Note:** Not all categories may be available for your region. Focus on Countryside and Tropical as the must-haves.
