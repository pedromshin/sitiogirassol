Title: "Content Batch Production Workflow: Sitio Girassol"
Date: March 2026

This document formalizes the batch production approach introduced in Phase 2's Instagram Content Strategy (Section 5) into a repeatable, time-boxed checklist the owner executes every 2 weeks.

**Sections:**

1. **Workflow Overview** - Per D-12: 5-step cycle diagram: Plan (30 min) -> Film (2-3 hrs) -> Edit (1.5-2 hrs) -> Schedule (30 min) -> Post and Review (15 min/day ongoing). Total per cycle: approximately 5-6 hours every 2 weeks. Emphasize this fits within 10-15 hrs/week constraint because filming happens in 2 concentrated sessions per month, not daily.

2. **Step 1: PLAN (30 minutes)** - Per D-12, D-13:
   - Open the Reel Templates Library (deliverables/reel-templates-expanded.md)
   - Select 6-8 concepts for this batch, covering all 4 content pillars in the correct ratio: 2 Property Experience (30%), 2 Local Discovery (25%), 1-2 Social Proof (25%), 1 Behind the Scenes (20%)
   - Check which concepts were used in the last 2 weeks -- never repeat the same concept back-to-back (per Research Pitfall 5 content fatigue prevention)
   - Write a simple shot list for the filming session on paper or phone notes
   - Check weather forecast -- plan outdoor shots for clear/golden-hour days
   - Planning checklist with [ ] checkboxes

3. **Step 2: FILM (2-3 hours)** - Per D-13, D-14:
   - Equipment: iPhone only, natural lighting, tripod or selfie stick for stabilization
   - Best times to film: golden hour (5:30-6:30 PM in autumn, 4:30-5:30 PM in winter) for Property Experience and sunset content; mornings (7-9 AM) for "Manha no Sitio" and garden shots; midday for pool and activity shots
   - Film in order of location (not concept) to minimize moving equipment: start at pool area -> move to BBQ area -> garden/nature -> interior rooms -> exterior/entrance
   - Capture 8-12 raw clips minimum (overshoot by 50% since not all clips will be usable)
   - Each clip: 5-15 seconds raw footage. Film horizontal AND vertical versions of key shots.
   - Filming checklist with [ ] checkboxes per location

4. **Step 3: EDIT (1.5-2 hours, ~15 min per Reel)** - Per D-15:
   - Tool: CapCut free tier
   - Workflow per Reel: Import clips -> Trim to 15-30 seconds total -> Add text overlays for hooks (first 3 seconds) -> Add trending or appropriate audio -> Review for watermarks -> Export at 1080p vertical (9:16)
   - WARNING: Only use templates and effects marked "Free" in CapCut. Pro templates embed permanent watermarks. Preview export before saving. (From Research Pitfall 4)
   - Delete CapCut end card before final export (adds branded outro)
   - Save exported Reels to a dedicated phone folder: "Girassol Reels - [Month]"
   - Editing checklist: [ ] per Reel with concept name

5. **Step 4: SCHEDULE (30 minutes)** - Per D-16:
   - Tool: Meta Business Suite (free, on desktop or mobile)
   - Upload all 6-8 edited Reels
   - Set posting times: consistent days (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sun per Phase 2 calendar), consistent times (7-9 AM or 6-8 PM Brasilia time)
   - Add captions from template library (copy/paste from reel-templates-expanded.md, customize as needed)
   - Add hashtags from template library (5-10 relevant hashtags, not 30)
   - LIMITATION: Meta Business Suite scheduling limited to 29 days ahead and 90-second Reels. If a Reel exceeds 90 seconds, post manually.
   - Scheduling checklist with [ ] checkboxes per Reel

6. **Step 5: POST and REVIEW (15 min/day ongoing)** - Per D-17:
   - Daily: Respond to comments within 2 hours during operating hours
   - Weekly (same day as ad performance review): Check Instagram Insights for each posted Reel:
     - Reach: Is it growing week over week?
     - 3-second hold rate: Target > 40%. If a concept drops below 40%, retire it temporarily (per Research Pitfall 5)
     - Saves and shares: Indicates high-quality content worth repurposing as ad creative for Layer 1
   - Flag top performers (> 2x average reach) for ad creative repurposing in the Meta Ads funnel
   - Weekly review checklist with [ ] checkboxes

7. **Bi-Monthly Cycle Calendar** - Visual 4-week calendar showing when each step happens:
   - Week 1: Film Session A (covers weeks 1-2 posting)
   - Week 2: Review Week 1 performance, adjust Week 2 scheduling if needed
   - Week 3: Film Session B (covers weeks 3-4 posting)
   - Week 4: Review and plan next month's concepts
   - Show total time investment per month: approximately 10-12 hours of content work, fitting within the 10-15 hrs/week total constraint

8. **Troubleshooting** - Common issues:
   - "I missed a filming session" -- post saved clips or user-generated content; never go dark for > 3 days
   - "My Reels aren't getting views" -- check hooks (are first 3 seconds compelling?), try different posting times, review hashtag relevance
   - "I'm running out of ideas" -- return to template library, vary the same concept with different weather/time/guests
   - Content fatigue mitigation: rotate templates, vary hooks, add 2-3 new templates every 2 months

Title: "Revenue Tracking Template: Sitio Girassol"
Date: March 2026

This document provides a Google Sheets template the owner copies and fills weekly to track revenue progress toward R$4,000 (April) and R$7,000 (May) targets.

**Sections:**

1. **Overview** - Purpose: single place to track all booking revenue, ad spend, and marketing ROI. The owner updates this once per week (same day as the ad performance review from the funnel guide). Takes approximately 10-15 minutes per week.

2. **Google Sheets Setup** - Step-by-step instructions:
   - Open Google Sheets at sheets.google.com
   - Create a new spreadsheet named "Sitio Girassol - Revenue Tracker 2026"
   - Create 3 tabs: "Weekly Data", "Monthly Summary", "Dashboard"

3. **Tab 1: Weekly Data** - Per D-23. Provide the exact column layout:
   - Column A: Week (format: "Apr W1", "Apr W2", "Apr W3", "Apr W4", "May W1", etc.)
   - Column B: Platform (Airbnb / Booking.com / VRBO / TemporadaLivre / Direct-WhatsApp)
   - Column C: Revenue (R$) -- total received for that platform that week
   - Column D: Nights Booked -- number of nights booked (not number of bookings)
   - Column E: ADR (R$) -- calculated: =C/D (Revenue / Nights). If D is 0, show "-"
   - Column F: Ad Spend (R$) -- total Meta Ads spend for the week (from Ads Manager billing)
   - Column G: Leads Generated -- number of WhatsApp messages received from ads
   - Column H: Source -- "Como voce nos encontrou?" response (Airbnb Search / Instagram / Facebook Ad / Google / Indicacao / Outro)
   - Provide 8 example rows of sample data covering 2 weeks across multiple platforms to show the owner exactly what a filled-in sheet looks like. Use realistic sample numbers:
     - Apr W1 / Airbnb / R$840 / 3 nights / R$280 / R$140 / 5 leads / "Airbnb Search"
     - Apr W1 / Direct-WhatsApp / R$560 / 2 nights / R$280 / (included above) / - / "Facebook Ad"
     - Apr W2 / Airbnb / R$1,120 / 4 nights / R$280 / R$140 / 7 leads / "Airbnb Search"
     - etc.

4. **Tab 2: Monthly Summary** - Per D-24, D-25. Provide the exact layout:
   - Row per month (April, May, June, etc.)
   - Columns: Month, Total Revenue, Total Nights, Average ADR, Total Ad Spend, Total Leads, Bookings from Ads, ROI, Cost per Booking, Cumulative Revenue, Target, Gap
   - Formulas (provide exact Google Sheets formulas):
     - Total Revenue: =SUMIFS('Weekly Data'!C:C, 'Weekly Data'!A:A, "*Apr*") -- sum revenue for all April weeks
     - Average ADR: =Total Revenue / Total Nights
     - ROI: =(Total Revenue - Total Ad Spend) / Total Ad Spend. Per D-25. Format as percentage.
     - Cost per Booking: =Total Ad Spend / Bookings from Ads. Per D-25.
     - Cumulative Revenue: =SUM of all months to date
     - Target: R$4,000 for April, R$7,000 cumulative by May
     - Gap: =Target - Cumulative Revenue. Conditional formatting: green if <= 0 (target met), red if > 0

5. **Tab 3: Dashboard** - Simple visual summary (optional but recommended):
   - Cell showing current cumulative revenue in large font
   - Cell showing April target (R$4,000) and progress percentage
   - Cell showing May target (R$7,000) and progress percentage
   - Cell showing current month's ROI
   - Simple bar chart of weekly revenue (if the owner is comfortable with Google Sheets charts -- provide instructions)

6. **How to Update Weekly** - Checklist for the weekly 10-15 minute update:
   - [ ] Log into each platform dashboard (Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO, TemporadaLivre)
   - [ ] Record revenue received this week per platform in Weekly Data tab
   - [ ] Record total nights booked per platform
   - [ ] Log into Meta Ads Manager > Billing > record total ad spend for the week
   - [ ] Count WhatsApp messages received from ads this week
   - [ ] For any new bookings: ask "Como voce nos encontrou?" and record in Source column
   - [ ] Check Monthly Summary tab: is cumulative revenue on track?
   - [ ] If Gap is > R$1,000 below target pace: consider increasing ad spend or adjusting funnel

7. **Attribution Guidance** - Per Research Pitfall 6:
   - Meta Pixel CANNOT track Airbnb/Booking.com conversions. Manual attribution is the only option.
   - Always ask guests "Como voce nos encontrou?" during first conversation
   - Accept approximate attribution -- the goal is directional insight (are ads generating leads that become bookings?), not precise per-channel ROAS
   - Compare weeks with ad spend to weeks without to see lift

8. **Template Ready to Copy** - Provide the complete column headers and sample data in a clean table format that the owner can manually recreate in Google Sheets. Include exact cell references for all formulas.
