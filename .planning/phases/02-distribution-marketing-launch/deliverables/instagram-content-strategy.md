# Instagram Content Strategy: Sitio Girassol

**Account:** @sitiogirassol.sp
**Date:** March 2026
**Objective:** Establish a sustainable 3-4 Reels per week cadence with defined content pillars, repeatable concepts, and batch production to build organic reach and generate booking inquiries.

---

## 1. Strategy Overview

### Account & Goals

- **Instagram account:** @sitiogirassol.sp (already active)
- **Posting cadence:** 3-4 Reels per week (per D-22, INSTA-01)
- **Primary goal:** Build organic reach and create content that drives booking inquiries via WhatsApp and Airbnb
- **Secondary goal:** Create content that can be repurposed as ad creative for Facebook/Instagram campaigns (see Facebook Ads Restructuring Guide)
- **Filming approach:** iPhone only (per D-24) -- no professional cameras or production equipment needed
- **Production model:** Batch production (per D-25) -- film 2-3 hours, produce 2 weeks of content

### Why Reels, Not Photos

Instagram's 2026 algorithm heavily favors Reels over static posts:

- **Watch time is the primary ranking signal** for Reels -- the longer people watch, the more Instagram shows your content to new audiences
- **3-second hold rate above 60%** outperforms content with hold rates below 40% by **5-10x in reach** -- this means your hook (first 3 seconds) is the single most important element
- **Instagram's 2026 redesign puts Reels front and center** -- the app layout prioritizes video content in discovery and explore feeds
- **Consistent posting on the same days** improves algorithmic reach -- Instagram rewards predictable content schedules

### Content Philosophy

**Show the EXPERIENCE, not the space.**

- A video of someone splashing into the pool sells more than a photo of an empty pool
- A Reel of meat sizzling on the churrasqueira sells more than a photo of a cold BBQ grill
- A sunset time-lapse with ambient sounds sells more than a daytime exterior photo
- Guest reactions and happy moments sell more than room tours

Every piece of content should make the viewer think: "I want to be there."

## 2. Content Pillars

Four content pillars define what you post and ensure variety. Each pillar serves a different purpose in the booking journey.

| Pillar | Allocation | Posts/Week | What It Covers | Why It Works |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Property Experience** | **30%** | 1 | Pool, BBQ, nature, mornings, sunsets, property highlights | Shows what guests will actually feel and experience -- the emotional pull |
| **Local Discovery** | **25%** | 1 | Sao Roque wineries, restaurants, trails, markets, road trip | Positions the sitio as a gateway to the region -- "book here, explore everything" |
| **Social Proof** | **25%** | 1 | Guest reactions, review highlights, happy moments, testimonials | Builds trust through real guest experiences -- people trust other guests more than the host |
| **Behind the Scenes** | **20%** | 0-1 | Pool cleaning, garden work, guest prep, hosting stories, property care | Creates personal connection and authenticity -- shows the care behind the experience |

### Pillar Rotation Rules

- **Property Experience** gets the most content (30%) because it directly drives desire to book
- **Local Discovery** and **Social Proof** are equal (25% each) because both address the "why should I choose this?" question from different angles
- **Behind the Scenes** is the lowest (20%) because it builds trust but does not directly drive bookings -- use it as a "filler" when you need a 4th post in a week
- **Never post the same pillar two days in a row** -- variety keeps your audience engaged
- **If you can only post 3 Reels one week**, drop Behind the Scenes first

## 5. Batch Filming Guide

### Why Batch?

Filming every day is unsustainable with 10-15 hours/week total for all property management. Instead, batch production means:

- **1 filming session (2-3 hours)** produces **8-12 raw clips**
- **8-12 raw clips** edit into **6-8 Reels**
- **6-8 Reels** = **2 weeks of content**
- Schedule all 6-8 Reels in advance using Meta Business Suite
- **Result:** You film twice a month, but post 3-4 times a week

### Filming Session Plan (2-3 hours)

```
Filming Session Checklist:

1. GOLDEN HOUR EXTERIORS (30 min) -- Covers: Concepts 1, 2, 7
   Schedule session around sunrise (6-7 AM) OR sunset (5-6 PM)
   [ ] Pool reflections in golden light
   [ ] Garden/nature in warm tones
   [ ] Balcony/veranda view
   [ ] Property exterior wide shot
   [ ] Coffee cup with nature backdrop (Concept 1)
   [ ] Walking approach to property (Concept 2)
   [ ] Sunset/sunrise time-lapse if possible (Concept 7)

2. POOL AREA (30 min) -- Covers: Concepts 1, 2, 6
   [ ] Wide shot of full pool area
   [ ] Close-up of water surface/reflections
   [ ] Someone swimming or feet dangling in water (if available)
   [ ] Pool edge details (towels, drinks)
   [ ] BBQ area adjacent to pool

3. INTERIOR WALKTHROUGH (20 min) -- Covers: Concepts 2, 5, 6
   [ ] Walking through each room in POV style
   [ ] Bedroom details: made beds, fresh towels, pillows
   [ ] Kitchen area
   [ ] Living/common area
   [ ] Bathroom (clean and styled)

4. BBQ/KITCHEN AREA (20 min) -- Covers: Concept 6
   [ ] Churrasqueira (BBQ grill) -- ideally with fire/food
   [ ] Food preparation shots
   [ ] Table set for a meal
   [ ] Close-up of sizzling meat or grilling (if possible)

5. LOCAL AREA (30 min, if time allows) -- Covers: Concept 3
   [ ] Drive-by shots of local attractions (wineries, restaurants)
   [ ] Quick clips at 3-4 locations
   [ ] Scenic views on the drive
   [ ] Road trip perspective (passenger-side filming)

6. BEHIND THE SCENES (20 min) -- Covers: Concept 5
   [ ] Cleaning in progress (vacuum, mop, pool net)
   [ ] Arranging details (flowers, welcome items)
   [ ] "Before" shots (messy/in-progress)
   [ ] "After" shots (pristine, ready for guests)
```

### Output Target

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Raw clips per session | 8-12 |
| Edited Reels per session | 6-8 |
| Content coverage | 2 weeks (at 3-4 Reels/week) |
| Filming sessions per month | 2 |
| Total filming time per month | 4-6 hours |
| Total editing time per month | 3-4 hours |
| **Total Instagram time per month** | **7-10 hours** |

### After Filming: Edit and Schedule

1. Transfer clips from iPhone to a dedicated **"Sitio Content"** folder (create this on your phone)
2. Edit each Reel using **Instagram's built-in editor** (or CapCut for more advanced editing -- free app)
3. Add text overlays, music, and transitions
4. Write captions using the templates from Section 4
5. Schedule all Reels for the next 2 weeks using **Meta Business Suite**
6. Set consistent posting times (recommended: 7-9 AM or 6-8 PM Brasilia time)

## 6. Filming Tips for iPhone

### Camera Settings

- **Film in portrait mode (vertical)** for all Reels -- 9:16 aspect ratio
- **Use 1080p resolution** (Settings > Camera > Record Video > 1080p at 30fps)
- **Enable grid lines** (Settings > Camera > Grid ON) to keep shots level
- **Lock exposure** by tapping and holding on the screen where you want to focus -- this prevents the camera from constantly readjusting brightness

### Lighting

- **Natural light is always best** -- film outdoors or near windows
- **Golden hour (sunrise/sunset)** produces the most visually striking footage
- **Avoid harsh midday sun** -- it creates unflattering shadows and washed-out colors
- **Overcast days** give soft, even lighting -- surprisingly good for property details

### Stability

- **Keep the camera steady** -- lean against a wall, tree, or railing
- A **cheap phone tripod** (R$30-50) is worth buying for time-lapses and static shots
- For walking shots (POV), hold the phone with both hands and walk slowly
- **Avoid zooming** -- move closer instead. Digital zoom degrades quality.

### Audio

- **Natural sounds are gold** -- birds, water, wind, fire crackling
- For Reels with music, you will add it in editing -- no need to capture perfect audio
- If you want to speak on camera, film in a quiet area (no wind, no traffic)

### General Tips

- **Film 2-3 takes of each shot** -- pick the best one later
- **Keep raw clips to 10-30 seconds each** -- shorter is better for Reels
- **Use Instagram's built-in editor** for text overlays, music, and transitions
- **Save ALL raw footage** to a "Sitio Content" folder organized by date -- you will reuse clips
- **Film in both morning and evening** to get variety in lighting

## 8. Content Repurposing

### Reels as Ad Creative

Your best-performing Reels can be repurposed as Facebook/Instagram ad creative for the Messages campaign (see Facebook Ads Restructuring Guide):

- **Ad Set 2 (Instagram Engagers)** uses a Video/Reel as Ad B
- Take your best-performing Reel (highest views, most engagement) and submit it as ad creative
- The Reel does not need to be re-filmed -- use the same video file
- Add a WhatsApp CTA button when setting up the ad

### Which Reels Work Best as Ads?

| Pillar | Works as Ad? | Why |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Property Experience | Excellent | Directly shows what the guest gets |
| Local Discovery | Good | Shows the region, positions the property as a base |
| Social Proof | Excellent | Guest reactions are authentic and persuasive |
| Behind the Scenes | Rarely | Too niche for cold audiences, better for organic followers |

### Stories from Reels

- Post Reel snippets (3-5 second clips) as Instagram Stories to drive views to the full Reel
- Use the "Share to Story" feature after posting a Reel
- Add a poll or question sticker: "Quem quer um fim de semana assim?" (engagement boost)

## 9. Performance Tracking

### Key Metrics to Monitor

Check these metrics weekly (Instagram Insights > Content > Reels):

| Metric | What It Tells You | Good Benchmark |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Views** | Total reach | Growing week over week |
| **Watch time** | Content quality | Average > 10 seconds |
| **3-second hold rate** | Hook effectiveness | > 60% |
| **Shares** | Content virality | More shares = more reach |
| **Saves** | Content value | People save content they plan to act on |
| **Profile visits from Reel** | Conversion interest | Higher = more booking potential |
| **Link clicks (from bio)** | Direct booking intent | Track via Instagram Insights |

### Monthly Review

At the end of each month, review:

1. **Which pillar got the most engagement?** Double down on winners.
2. **Which Reel concept got the most views?** Film more variations of it.
3. **Which posting day/time performed best?** Adjust schedule accordingly.
4. **How many profile visits came from Reels?** This is your "booking funnel" metric.
5. **Any Reels worth repurposing as ad creative?** Flag the top 2-3 for the next ad refresh.

## 10. Guest Permission Process for Social Proof Content

### Check-In Script

When guests arrive, include this in your welcome conversation:

> "Sejam bem-vindos ao Sitio Girassol! Uma coisa rapida -- a gente posta conteudo no Instagram sobre o sitio. Se voces se sentirem confortaveis, adorariamos filmar uns momentos da estadia de voces para compartilhar. So se quiserem, sem pressao nenhuma!"

### Rules

- **Never film without asking first**
- **Never post without explicit verbal or written permission**
- **If guests say no, respect it immediately** -- do not ask again
- **If guests say yes, confirm what is OK:**
  - Can we show your faces?
  - Can we tag you?
  - Can we use the footage in ads (in addition to organic posts)?
- **Keep it casual** -- guests should feel excited to be featured, not pressured
- **Offer to tag them** -- many guests love being featured and will share the content with their own followers (free reach)

### After Posting

- Send the Reel to the guest via WhatsApp: "Olha como ficou! Obrigado por serem parte do nosso conteudo"
- This often leads to the guest sharing it (more reach) and leaving a positive review
